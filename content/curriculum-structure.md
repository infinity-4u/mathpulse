# Curriculum Structure — Australian Maths App

## Years 7–10: ACARA Australian Curriculum (Version 9)
Consistent across all states and territories. Content description codes use the format AC9M[Year][Strand][Number], e.g. AC9M7N01.

Strand abbreviations: N = Number, A = Algebra, M = Measurement, SP = Space, ST = Statistics, P = Probability

---

### Year 7

**Number**
- AC9M7N01 — Describe the relationship between perfect square numbers and square roots; investigating square roots using square tiles
- AC9M7N02 — Represent natural numbers in expanded notation using powers of 10; comparing representations
- AC9M7N03 — Index notation with positive integers; representing numbers as products of prime factors
- AC9M7N04 — Fractions, decimals, percentages — representations and conversions
- AC9M7N05 — Round decimals to a given accuracy; estimate results of computations
- AC9M7N06 — Operations with integers — including negative numbers
- AC9M7N07 — Addition and subtraction of fractions with related denominators
- AC9M7N08 — Multiplication and division of fractions
- AC9M7N09 — Calculate a percentage of a quantity; express quantities as percentages

**Algebra**
- AC9M7A01 — Formulate algebraic expressions using variables; substitution
- AC9M7A02 — Solve simple linear equations
- AC9M7A03 — Describe patterns and relationships using graphs, tables, words, and equations
- AC9M7A04 — Recognise and use properties of equality and operations

**Measurement**
- AC9M7M01 — Solve problems involving the perimeter and area of rectangles and composite shapes
- AC9M7M02 — Solve problems involving the volume of prisms
- AC9M7M03 — Duration, 12- and 24-hour time, time zones
- AC9M7M04 — Convert between metric units of length, area, volume, and capacity

**Space**
- AC9M7SP01 — Properties of angles: complementary, supplementary, vertically opposite, co-interior, alternate, corresponding
- AC9M7SP02 — Classify triangles and quadrilaterals; properties and relationships
- AC9M7SP03 — Describe and classify 3D objects; cross-sections and nets
- AC9M7SP04 — Perform and describe translations, reflections, and rotations

**Statistics**
- AC9M7ST01 — Acquire data sets; plan and conduct statistical investigations
- AC9M7ST02 — Create and compare different data displays; misleading graphs
- AC9M7ST03 — Calculate mean, median, mode, and range; interpret results

**Probability**
- AC9M7P01 — Describe probability of events using fractions, decimals, and percentages
- AC9M7P02 — Conduct probability experiments; compare experimental and theoretical probability

### Year 8

**Number**
- AC9M8N01 — Rational and irrational numbers; representing on number line
- AC9M8N02 — Expand, factorise, and simplify algebraic expressions involving positive and negative integers
- AC9M8N03 — Operations with fractions, decimals, and percentages — all four operations
- AC9M8N04 — Ratio and proportion; solving proportion problems
- AC9M8N05 — Financial mathematics — profit, loss, discount, GST, simple interest

**Algebra**
- AC9M8A01 — Simplify algebraic expressions; collect like terms; expand and factorise
- AC9M8A02 — Solve linear equations with variables on both sides
- AC9M8A03 — Solve linear inequalities and graph on a number line
- AC9M8A04 — Graph linear relationships; gradient and y-intercept; interpret graphs

**Measurement**
- AC9M8M01 — Area of triangles, parallelograms, trapezoids, rhombuses, kites, and composite shapes
- AC9M8M02 — Area and circumference of circles
- AC9M8M03 — Volume and surface area of prisms and cylinders
- AC9M8M04 — Solve problems involving duration using 12- and 24-hour time across time zones

**Space**
- AC9M8SP01 — Congruence of triangles — conditions (SSS, SAS, AAS, RHS)
- AC9M8SP02 — Properties of similar figures; scale factors
- AC9M8SP03 — Transformation combinations; identify symmetry
- AC9M8SP04 — Plot and read coordinates in all four quadrants; midpoint and distance

**Statistics**
- AC9M8ST01 — Sampling methods; representative samples vs biased samples
- AC9M8ST02 — Analyse and compare data sets using back-to-back stem-and-leaf plots and box plots
- AC9M8ST03 — Mean, median, mode, range, and outliers; effect of outliers

**Probability**
- AC9M8P01 — Complementary events; sum of probabilities equals 1
- AC9M8P02 — Two-step probability experiments; lists, arrays, tree diagrams

### Year 9

**Number**
- AC9M9N01 — Real numbers — surds, irrational numbers, exact vs approximate values
- AC9M9N02 — Scientific notation; very large and very small numbers
- AC9M9N03 — Financial mathematics — compound interest, depreciation

**Algebra**
- AC9M9A01 — Expand and factorise quadratic expressions
- AC9M9A02 — Solve quadratic equations by factorisation
- AC9M9A03 — Sketch and interpret parabolas; key features (vertex, axis of symmetry, intercepts)
- AC9M9A04 — Non-linear relationships; identify from tables, graphs, and equations
- AC9M9A05 — Simultaneous linear equations — graphical and algebraic methods

**Measurement**
- AC9M9M01 — Surface area and volume of pyramids, cones, and spheres
- AC9M9M02 — Applications of Pythagoras' theorem — 2D and simple 3D problems
- AC9M9M03 — Trigonometry — right-angled triangles; sin, cos, tan ratios and applications

**Space**
- AC9M9SP01 — Properties of parallel lines and transversals; proofs of angle relationships
- AC9M9SP02 — Properties of quadrilaterals — proof using congruence
- AC9M9SP03 — Scale drawings and maps; applying similarity

**Statistics**
- AC9M9ST01 — Data distributions — shape, centre, spread; comparing two groups
- AC9M9ST02 — Scatter plots; line of best fit; correlation (not causation)
- AC9M9ST03 — Evaluate statistical reports; identify potential bias and misuse of data

**Probability**
- AC9M9P01 — Relative frequency as an estimate of probability from large data sets
- AC9M9P02 — Two-way tables and Venn diagrams; conditional probability (informal)

### Year 10

**Number**
- AC9M10N01 — Real and irrational numbers; operations with surds (simplify, add, multiply)
- AC9M10N02 — Financial mathematics — annuities, loan repayments (introductory)

**Algebra**
- AC9M10A01 — Factorise quadratic expressions including difference of two squares and perfect squares
- AC9M10A02 — Solve quadratic equations using the quadratic formula and completing the square
- AC9M10A03 — Sketch and interpret graphs of parabolas, exponentials, and circles
- AC9M10A04 — Solve systems of equations including one linear and one non-linear
- AC9M10A05 — Logarithms — introduction to logarithmic scale; log laws (optional enrichment)

**Measurement**
- AC9M10M01 — Surface area and volume — composite solids; optimisation problems
- AC9M10M02 — Trigonometry — applications in 2D and 3D including angles of elevation and depression, bearings
- AC9M10M03 — Sine rule and cosine rule for non-right-angled triangles; area formula

**Space**
- AC9M10SP01 — Geometric proofs using congruence and similarity
- AC9M10SP02 — Circle geometry — chord, tangent, and angle properties (introductory)
- AC9M10SP03 — Vectors — introduction; addition, subtraction, scalar multiplication (optional enrichment)

**Statistics**
- AC9M10ST01 — Bivariate data — use technology to find line of best fit; interpret slope and intercept
- AC9M10ST02 — Evaluate and compare statistical reports; sampling variability
- AC9M10ST03 — Standard deviation — interpretation and comparison of data sets

**Probability**
- AC9M10P01 — Conditional probability — formal definition; independent and dependent events
- AC9M10P02 — Permutations and combinations — counting techniques; applications to probability

## Years 11–12: State Senior Secondary Pathways (V2)

Not in V1 scope. Documented here for planning purposes.

The Australian senior secondary curriculum is not nationally consistent — each state sets its own syllabus. Key pathways by state:

### NSW (NESA)
- Mathematics Standard 1 (HSC, no ATAR)
- Mathematics Standard 2 (HSC, ATAR)
- Mathematics Advanced (HSC, ATAR) — the most common pathway
- Mathematics Extension 1 (HSC, ATAR)
- Mathematics Extension 2 (HSC, ATAR)

### VIC (VCAA)
- Foundation Mathematics (no ATAR)
- General Mathematics (ATAR)
- Mathematical Methods (ATAR)
- Specialist Mathematics (ATAR)
- Further Mathematics (ATAR) — very commonly taken alongside Methods

### QLD (QCAA)
- Essential Mathematics
- General Mathematics
- Mathematical Methods
- Specialist Mathematics

### WA (SCSA)
- Mathematics Foundations
- Mathematics Essential
- Mathematics Applications
- Mathematics Methods
- Mathematics Specialist

### SA, TAS, ACT, NT
Generally follow similar pathway structures to above with local variations.

**V2 recommendation**: Start with NSW (largest student population) and VIC (second largest), covering Mathematical Methods and Mathematical Advanced/Methods respectively, as these are the highest-enrolment senior pathways nationally.

## Content priority for V1 question bank

Based on NAPLAN data and common teacher feedback, highest-priority substrands to author first:

1. Year 7–8 Number (fractions, percentages, integers) — most commonly assessed, highest failure rates
2. Year 9 Algebra (quadratics, simultaneous equations) — common gateway to senior maths
3. Year 9–10 Measurement (Pythagoras, trigonometry) — high exam weighting
4. Year 10 Algebra (quadratic formula, graphs) — essential for Year 11 readiness

Low priority for initial bank: Year 7 Space (less examined), Year 10 Circle Geometry (small cohort, complex to write)
